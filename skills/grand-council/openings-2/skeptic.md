# Skeptic Seat

**Artifact F**
**Motion to Reject** Artifact F on C7 ("mobile sat navigation"). The L-band is allocated for GNSS *ranging* (e.g., GPS L1 at 1575.42 MHz), not communication, making the band-vs-application mapping functionally misleading. Further, C5 ("low latency") omits the necessary condition of massive constellation deployment for non-stationary orbits, a fatal omission regarding the launch-cost/latency tradeoff. C7 Ka-band "high-speed internet" is overly charitable, failing to account for rain fade and atmospheric absorption. 

**Artifact G**
**Falsifiable counter to C2:** The assertion of a definitive 2000-4000 transitional regime is pedagogically unstable; critical Reynolds number onset varies (some canonical texts cite 2100 or 2300). The sharp boundaries presented are approximations, not invariants. Otherwise, conceded.

**Artifact H**
**Falsifiable counter to C1:** "Higher \(r\) generally improves efficiency" masks a hard physical boundary: in SI engines, \(r\) is strictly bounded by detonation (knock), typically capping at \(r \le 12\) for pump gas. The word "generally" obscures catastrophic failure modes. 
**Falsifiable counter to C3:** The Diesel formula assumes a constant cutoff ratio \(\rho\), whereas real diesel operation exhibits a variable \(\rho\) dependent on load/throttle position.

**Artifact I**
**Concede C3:** 1 TR is exactly 3.5168525 kW, making 3.517 a defensible 4-sig-fig rounding. The 12,000 Btu/h conversion is 11,999.98, an acceptable engineering convenience. Artifact conceded.
