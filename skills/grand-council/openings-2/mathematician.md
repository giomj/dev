# The Mathematician's Openings: Artifacts F, G, H, I

## Artifact F
Regarding C4, GEO altitude \( r \) derives structurally from Kepler's third law: \( r = (GM_E T^2 / 4\pi^2)^{1/3} \). Setting the sidereal period \( T = 86164 \) s yields an orbital radius \( r \approx 42,164 \) km. Subtracting the Earth's radius (\( R_E \)) gives an altitude of approximately 35,786 km. The mathematical structure is sound. For C6, GHz frequency ranges map correctly to \( 10^9 \) Hz units, satisfying dimensional consistency. On C7, note that the mapping from frequency band to application is a many-to-many relation, not a well-defined function. Otherwise, I abstain — applied topology is outside my charge.

## Artifact G
Regarding C1, Bernoulli's equation is a first integral of Euler's equation along a streamline. For C2, the Reynolds transition boundaries are empirical (\( Re_c \sim 2300 \) for pipes classically), not formal derivations, though 2000 is a common pedagogical rounding. On C3, continuity \( A_1V_1 = A_2V_2 \) is the 1D specialization of the flux integral \( \int \rho \mathbf{v} \cdot d\mathbf{A} = \text{const} \). For C4, Pascal's relation \( F_1/A_1 = F_2/A_2 \) intrinsically assumes quasi-static equilibrium.

## Artifact H
Regarding C2, the Otto efficiency \( \eta = 1 - r^{1-\gamma} \) is formally derived from the air-standard cycle (constant \( c_v \), ideal gas, reversible adiabatic compression). For C3, the Diesel formula \( \eta_D = 1 - \frac{1}{r^{\gamma-1}} \cdot \frac{\rho^\gamma - 1}{\gamma(\rho - 1)} \) is correct. We can verify its structural integrity: taking the limit as the cutoff ratio \( \rho \to 1 \) recovers the Otto efficiency via L'Hôpital's rule. For C4, MEP is fundamentally an integral quantity defined by \( \text{MEP} = \frac{\oint P dV}{V_{\text{swept}}} \).

## Artifact I
Regarding C1, \( \text{COP}_R = Q_L / W \) is structurally defined by the first law energy balance. The artifact completely omits the Carnot theoretical upper bound, \( \text{COP}_{R,\text{Carnot}} = T_L / (T_H - T_L) \). For C2, \( \text{RE} = h_1 - h_4 \) properly leverages the steady-flow energy equation \( Q = \Delta h \) at constant pressure.
