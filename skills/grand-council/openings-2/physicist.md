## Artifact F
C4 is sound: Kepler gives \(T=2\pi\sqrt{r^3/\mu}\Rightarrow r=(\mu T^2/4\pi^2)^{1/3}\). With \(T=86164\,s\), \(\mu_E=3.986\times10^{14}\,m^3s^{-2}\), \(r=4.2164\times10^7\,m\); subtract \(R_E=6.378\times10^6\,m\Rightarrow h=3.5786\times10^7\,m=35,786\,km\). “Fixed” requires circular equatorial prograde orbit, not merely that altitude. C5: low latency is physically justified: minimum vertical propagation \(h/c\) is \(0.5–6.7\,ms\) for 160–2000 km LEO, versus \(119\,ms\) ground-to-GEO; ground-GEO-ground doubles that before routing. “High speed” is not implied by altitude alone; bandwidth, EIRP, antenna gain, coding, and SNR set capacity. C6: L/C/Ku match IEEE radar letter bands; Ka as 26–40 GHz is wrong against IEEE: Ka starts at 27 GHz, so 26–27 GHz is not Ka.

## Artifact G
C1 is sound only with the hidden hypotheses stated: steady, incompressible, inviscid flow along a streamline, no shaft work/viscous loss; dimension check: \(P/(\rho g),V^2/(2g),z\) all have units of length. For real pipes add loss head: \(P/\rho g+V^2/2g+z+h_p-h_t-h_L=const\). C2: \(Re=\rho V D/\mu\) is dimensionless; the 2000/4000 thresholds are empirical regimes, not conservation-law discontinuities. Transition depends on roughness, inlet disturbance, geometry. C3 is sound for steady incompressible one-dimensional flow: \(\dot m=\rho AV\Rightarrow A_1V_1=A_2V_2\). C6 is sound: cavitation begins when local static pressure falls below \(p_v(T)\); in pump language insufficient NPSH lets vapor form, then collapse in higher-pressure regions, causing pitting/noise/performance loss.

## Artifact H
C1 is dimensionally sound: \(r=(V_s+V_c)/V_c=V_1/V_2\). “Higher \(r\) improves efficiency” is sound for the ideal cycles but bounded physically: SI engines by knock/end-gas autoignition, and all engines by heat transfer, friction, materials, emissions, and finite combustion time. C2 is sound only under air-standard Otto assumptions: ideal gas, internally reversible compression/expansion, constant \(\gamma\), constant-volume heat addition; \(\eta=1-r^{1-\gamma}\). Real \(\gamma(T)\), dissociation, pumping, and combustion losses move it. C3 likewise is air-standard Diesel with constant \(\gamma\) and cutoff ratio \(\rho=V_3/V_2\); not a universal real-engine efficiency. C4 is correct dimensionally: \(MEP=W_{cycle}/V_s\) has Pa. C5: \(BP=T(2\pi N/60)\) is watts if \(N\) is rpm; \(BP<IP\) by friction/pumping losses.

## Artifact I

C1 then gives \(COP_R=q_L/w_{in}=(h_1-h_4)/(h_2-h_1)\), dimensionless because kJ/kg cancels; “higher is better” is thermodynamically sound for same lift. C3: \(1\,TR=12000\,Btu/h=3.51685\,kW\), so 3.517 kW is a correct rounding; \(211\,kJ/min\) is also consistent. C4 is the standard vapor-compression energy loop.
