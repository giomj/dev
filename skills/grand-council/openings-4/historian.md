# Grand Council — Session 4 — Historian-Philosopher Opening

*Seat: Historian-Philosopher. Convened 2026-07-18, New Braunfels, TX. On the distribution posture — C1..C4 — of the session-3 reference shelf (n2yo · SatDump · GOGPT).*

**The public reference shelf and its lineage (C1).** To publish a shelf "to the masses" is to enter a lineage, not invent a genre. It runs from Diderot and d'Alembert's *Encyclopédie* — cross-referenced knowledge as public power — through Wikipedia (2001) and its demand that every claim be *sourced*, to the open-data commons: OpenStreetMap (2004), the IPCC data catalogs, Zenodo's DOI-minted deposits at CERN. Their common obligation is not "free stuff" but that **provenance travels with the artifact** — encyclopédistes footnoted, Zenodo stamps a permanent identifier and license, OSM carries its ODbL attribution. C1 — three regimes (proprietary n2yo, copyleft SatDump, CC BY 4.0 GOGPT) in one public repo — is sound *only if* attribution and upstream-ToS respect are enforced at build time, as the Engineer's `LICENSES.md` and CI attribution check provide. The lineage does not forbid mixed licenses; it forbids *silent* ones.

**The reader's failure modes (C1, C4).** Publishing to the masses means designing for the reader's predictable failures, not the expert's care. Three must be named. **(a)** The reader treats the aggregator as source — citing n2yo as if it were the USSPACECOM catalog it merely couriers; the shelf marks n2yo *as aggregator* on every surface, carrying the NORAD ID and TLE epoch age. **(b)** The reader strips attribution from CC BY 4.0 material — the commonest commons-violation, the reason CC and ODbL are enforceable instruments and not mere etiquette; the build-time check that fails any GOGPT-derived table lacking its attribution string is technical enforcement of a moral duty. **(c)** The reader assumes actively-maintained means correct — the WxToImg lesson inverted: a live tracker can serve a stale TLE, a fresh release a phantom "proposed" plant. The shelf answers with epoch age, status-weighting, and capacity-factor discipline surfaced, never hidden.

**Shelf versus service (C2).** C2 is the heart of the session, and it is ethical, not merely operational. To *run a service* is to become the courier of record — assuming liability for uptime and freshness, inviting the reader to be a passive consumer. To *publish a shelf* — catalogs, schemas, scripts, each consumer running its own ingest against upstream — shifts the reader from consumer to **co-vetter** and re-seats liability with the party holding the upstream relationship and its ToS. This is the move free software made in choosing the license over the binary, and CelesTrak made in 1985 by distributing element sets rather than a locked terminal. It refuses the paternalism of the black box: it hands the reader the footnote, not just the answer.

**The dashboard as public reading room (C4).** A pplx.app dashboard is a **reading room over an archive, not a replacement for it** — the display case whose duty is to point back to the stacks. Every view must carry source-with-aggregator-marked, TLE epoch age, GOGPT status breakdown, and the CC BY 4.0 attribution string. A reading room that hides its shelf-marks is a museum forgery; C4 enforces C1's promise at the point of contact with the masses.

**Stance.** C1 sound (mixed licenses coexist iff attribution + ToS are enforced at build time); C2 sound and ethical (shelf-not-service moves the reader from consumer to co-vetter); C3 sound (the shelf *is* the SDK); C4 sound and binding (the reading room must always cite back to the archive).
