## Artifact J — n2yo

n2yo is physically useful because it exposes current TLEs and SGP4-propagated positions, but SGP4 is a semi-analytical propagation of a fitted element set, not an orbit-determination product with meter-scale truth. For LEO objects such as the ISS, the TLE epoch matters strongly: the captured ISS element is epoch 2026 day 199.08891132, and atmospheric drag plus maneuvers make the error grow from near-epoch kilometer-scale operational usefulness toward stale, non-authoritative predictions as days pass. I would trust n2yo for “what is above me,” pass timing, pointing, and radio/visual planning over the API’s 300-second position window and 10-day pass window, but not for meter-scale ranging, conjunction assessment, geodesy, or instrument validation. The captured ISS TLE is physically coherent: inclination 51.6318° fixes the station’s ground-track latitude band, eccentricity 0.0006793 makes the orbit nearly circular, and mean motion 15.49037991 rev/day implies a period of about 92.96 minutes. Using Earth’s gravitational parameter, that mean motion corresponds to a semi-major axis of about 6,797.7 km and a rough altitude of about 420 km, with a spherical perigee/apogee estimate of about 415–424 km.

## Artifact K — SatDump

SatDump’s measurement chain is real physics, not merely software plumbing: an electromagnetic wave reaches an antenna, the SDR front end mixes and samples it into I/Q baseband, demodulation converts noisy samples into soft symbols, synchronization/FEC/deframing recover frames, and payload decoders turn the resulting bits into images or instrument products. SNR loss enters before and during sampling through antenna gain, polarization mismatch, free-space path loss, atmospheric/weather effects, receiver noise figure, gain setting, frequency error, Doppler, filtering, and interference. Quantization loss enters at the ADC and file/sample representation level, especially when using lower-resolution formats such as cs8/cu8/w8 rather than cf32, and further loss can be introduced by resampling, clipping, DC offset, IQ imbalance, or mistuned timing/carrier loops. The “pipeline” abstraction preserves the physics if its graph carries the physical parameters—sample rate, center frequency, modulation, symbol rate, Doppler handling, FEC, frame format, and instrument geometry—rather than pretending that bytes appear without a link budget. I therefore regard SatDump’s demodulate → deframe → decode → image-compose → project → geo-reference model as a defensible high-level representation of the receive chain, with the caveat that pipeline success is still bounded by the RF measurement quality.

## Artifact L — GOGPT

GOGPT’s plant-level CO₂ calculation is physically meaningful only if the capacity inventory is converted into energy and fuel burn: annual emissions require nameplate capacity, capacity factor, hours per year, thermal efficiency or heat rate, fuel carbon content/emission factor, and operating status. Lifetime CO₂ then adds a time integral over assumed service life, retirements, commissioning dates, and utilization, so it is a scenario-dependent physical estimate rather than a directly observed quantity. The headline 1,047 GW of global in-development oil/gas capacity is a real summed plant-level nameplate power figure, including proposed, permitted, and under-construction units, but capacity is not generation and therefore not CO₂. The USA 252 GW and China 153 GW subtotals likewise describe possible electrical power capability, not annual energy production or build certainty; the intake’s IRP warning is physically important because a filing artifact cannot be treated as steel, turbines, fuel flow, or stack emissions. To convert the 1,047 GW figure into annual generation one must assume a capacity factor—for example, 50% would imply about 4,586 TWh/year before any emissions factor is applied—then split oil versus gas and plant type to compute actual CO₂.

## Artifact M — Integration

The n2yo ↔ gravitational-compass link is the strongest physics link because real TLEs provide Earth-orbit trajectories that any gravity/propagation model must approximately reproduce, though they are SGP4 mean elements rather than meter-grade ground truth and must be handled in the correct Earth-centered reference frames. The intake phrase “heliocentric propagation cross-check” should be narrowed: ISS TLEs test near-Earth orbital dynamics, drag-sensitive propagation, frame transforms, and observation geometry, not a pure heliocentric gravity model by themselves. The grid-and-chain-mobile ↔ n2yo API + SatDump link is also physically defensible because pass prediction, line of sight, Doppler, RF reception, I/Q sampling, and payload decoding form one continuous measurement chain for “what is above me” and “can I receive it.” The conceptual-engineering-references ↔ GOGPT link is defensible for energy-systems physics, CO₂ accounting, capacity-factor reasoning, and stranded-asset scenarios, especially with the catalog thresholds of ≥50 MW globally and ≥20 MW in the EU+UK. The recursive-state-dynamics ↔ GOGPT link is weak physically: plant states such as proposed, permitted, operating, and retired are useful discrete transitions, but they become physics-relevant only if coupled to power, fuel flow, heat rate, emissions, and lifetime operation rather than treated as abstract labels. The force-carriers ↔ none ruling is correct, because oil/gas plant inventories and satellite downlink tools do not naturally instantiate a Standard Model reference artifact.
