### Artifact A
The ODEs are stated correctly: \(\dot x=\sigma(y-x)\), \(\dot y=x(\rho-z)-y\), \(\dot z=xy-\beta z\) (C1). The parameter roles are also right: \(\sigma\) is the Prandtl number, \(\rho\) is the Rayleigh-type control parameter, and \(\beta\) is the geometric factor; \((10,28,8/3)\) is the canonical chaotic case (C2). Deterministic sensitive dependence and the two-lobed strange attractor are physically and mathematically standard (C3, C4). I object only to wording in C5: Lorenz’s 1972 AAAS talk was titled “Does the flap of a butterfly’s wings in Brazil set off a tornado in Texas?”, but the title—and hence popular naming—is often credited to Philip Merilees, not Lorenz himself.

### Artifact B
I abstain — outside my charge. At most: Hermitian symmetry matters in quantum mechanics because observables are represented by self-adjoint operators, but C1–C3 are pure linear algebra, not physics.

### Artifact C
Reject the physics. \(H_0 t_u=1\) is not a “fundamental identity” if \(t_u\equiv1/H_0\); it is a tautology by definition (C1). \(A_0=cH_0\) has dimensions of acceleration, but dimensional legality is not dynamics; its scale resembles the MOND acceleration coincidence \(a_0\sim cH_0\) known since Milgrom 1983, so C2 needs attribution and mechanism, not numerology. The proposed \(H(t)=H_0(1-e^{-t/t_u})\) is cosmologically wrong: it rises from zero, whereas ΛCDM has \(H(t)\to\infty\) toward the radiation/matter-era Big Bang and tends to a constant only in the late de Sitter limit (C3, C5). Eleven significant figures for \(H_0\) are indefensible given the Planck–SH0ES-scale tension, about \(67.4\) versus \(73.0\) km/s/Mpc; C7 is false precision.

### Artifact D
The tank check is straightforward: \(\omega_0=1/\sqrt{LC}\), so with a 6-turn 6 mm air coil plausibly \(L\sim1\text{–}2\,\mu\text{H}\) and \(C\approx6\times0.33\,\mu\text{F}\approx2\,\mu\text{F}\), \(f_0=1/(2\pi\sqrt{LC})\sim80\text{–}160\,\text{kHz}\) (C1). The 1 kW label is not impossible but is unproven: at 48 V, \(I=P/V\approx1000/48=20.8\) A rail current. If each IRFP250N sees that current, \(I^2R\approx(21)^2(0.085)\approx37\) W per FET conduction loss before switching, diode, choke, and tank losses (C2, C5). That is heatsink-and-airflow territory. Asymmetric 12 V/22 V gate clamps, 10 A chokes, and absent cooling/snubbers make C3, C4, and C6 unsupported for continuous 1 kW service.

### Artifact E
[Synthesist-drafted; Physicist seat had already closed when Artifact E was added.] Q = A·v (C1) and A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ (C2) are correct in the limit of steady, incompressible, one-dimensional flow with a flat velocity profile. The stated cause — mass conservation, ∂ρ/∂t + ∇·(ρv) = 0 collapsing to ∇·v = 0 for constant ρ (C3) — is exactly right. The garden-hose intuition is honest. The highway analogy (C4) is where the physics breaks: real traffic is a *compressible* medium in the LWR sense, so a narrower road generally lowers velocity and raises density, not the reverse. Pedagogically useful; physically inexact past introductory framing.
