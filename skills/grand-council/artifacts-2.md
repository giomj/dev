## Artifact G — Fluid Mechanics slide 11 (Sanjiv Ganjir)
- **Intake.** Hand-drawn engineering notebook slide covering Bernoulli, Reynolds number, Continuity, Pascal's Law, Venturi meter, Cavitation.
- **Evidence tier.** Textbook.
- **Claims Under Review.**
  - C1. Bernoulli: P/(ρg) + V²/(2g) + z = constant along a streamline for ideal flow.
  - C2. Re = ρVD/μ; pipe flow laminar if Re < 2000, transitional 2000<Re<4000, turbulent Re > 4000.
  - C3. Continuity: A₁V₁ = A₂V₂ for incompressible steady flow.
  - C4. Pascal: pressure applied to confined fluid transmitted equally; F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂; P₁ = P₂; basis of hydraulic systems.
  - C5. Venturi meter is a differential-pressure device for discharge measurement; pressure drop at throat.
  - C6. Cavitation: bubble formation/growth/collapse when local pressure falls below vapor pressure; causes noise, pitting, performance loss.

## Artifact H — IC Engines slide 9 (Sanjiv Ganjir)
- **Intake.** Hand-drawn slide covering Compression ratio, Otto cycle, Diesel cycle, MEP, Brake Power, Volumetric Efficiency, with P-V diagrams.
- **Evidence tier.** Textbook.
- **Claims Under Review.**
  - C1. Compression ratio r = (V_s + V_c) / V_c; higher r "generally improves efficiency."
  - C2. Otto cycle efficiency η = 1 − 1/r^(γ−1); ideal cycle for spark-ignition; heat addition at constant volume.
  - C3. Diesel cycle efficiency η_D = 1 − (1/r^(γ−1)) · [(ρ^γ − 1) / (γ(ρ − 1))]; heat addition at constant pressure.
  - C4. MEP = Work per cycle / swept volume; size-independent performance parameter.
  - C5. Brake Power BP = 2πNT/60; measured on dynamometer; BP < IP.
  - C6. Volumetric efficiency η_v = actual intake volume / swept volume; affected by speed and valve timing.

## Artifact I — Refrigeration & Air Conditioning slide 10 (Sanjiv Ganjir)

- **Evidence tier.** Textbook.
- **Claims Under Review.**
  - C1. COP_R = RE / W where RE is refrigeration effect (kJ/kg) and W is compressor work input (kJ/kg); higher COP is better.
  - C2. RE = h₁ − h₄ in VCRS (evaporator exit enthalpy minus expansion-device exit enthalpy).
  - C3. 1 TR = 3.517 kW = 12,000 Btu/h = 3024 kcal/h = 211 kJ/min; melts 1 ton of ice in 24 h.
  - C4. VCC consists of compressor, condenser, expansion device, evaporator; plot on p–h chart.
  - C5. Psychrometry studies moist-air properties; key terms DBT, WBT, DPT, φ (RH), ω (humidity ratio), h (enthalpy).
  - C6. Relative humidity φ = P_v / P_ws × 100%.
